Chapter 11: Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter

               

1. Electron Emission

       

Process of emission of electrons from metal surface.

       
            Types:
            1. Thermionic Emission: By heating
            2. Field Emission: By strong electric field
            3. Photoelectric Emission: By light
            Work Function: $\phi_0 = h\nu_0$ = Minimum energy needed        
       

2. Photoelectric Effect

       

Emission of electrons when light of suitable frequency falls on metal surface.

               

Experimental Observations:

       
            1. Instantaneous process, no time lag
            2. For each metal, threshold frequency $\nu_0$ exists
            3. Above $\nu_0$, photoelectric current $\propto$ Intensity
            4. Max K.E $\propto$ frequency, independent of intensity        
       

Einstein's Photoelectric Equation

       
            $$h\nu = \phi_0 + K_{max}$$
            $$K_{max} = h\nu - h\nu_0 = eV_0$$
            $V_0$ = Stopping potential
            $h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34} Js$ = Planck's constant        
       
            Note: Photoelectric effect proves particle nature of light. Light consists of photons.        
       

Graphs:

       
            1. $K_{max}$ vs $\nu$: Straight line, slope = $h$
            2. $V_0$ vs $\nu$: Straight line, slope = $h/e$
            3. Photo current vs Intensity: Straight line passing origin
            4. Photo current vs Collector potential: Saturation current $\propto$ intensity        
       

3. Photon

       

Packet of energy. Particle nature of light.

       
            Energy: $$E = h\nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
            Momentum: $$p = \frac{E}{c} = \frac{h\nu}{c} = \frac{h}{\lambda}$$
            Rest mass = 0, Speed = $c$ in vacuum        
       

4. de Broglie Hypothesis

       

Matter also shows wave nature like light. Wave-particle duality.

       
            de Broglie wavelength: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$
            For electron accelerated by V volts: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}} = \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{V}} \text{Å}$$
            For thermal neutrons: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{3mkT}}$$        
       

5. Davisson and Germer Experiment

       

Experimental proof of wave nature of electrons.

       
            Electrons diffracted by Ni crystal like X-rays.
            Bragg's condition: $$2d\sin\theta = n\lambda$$
            Experimental $\lambda$ matched de Broglie $\lambda$.
            Confirmed: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$

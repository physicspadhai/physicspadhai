Chapter 4: Moving Charges and Magnetism

               

1. Magnetic Force - Lorentz Force

       

Force experienced by a charge moving in magnetic and electric field.

       
            $$\vec{F} = q[\vec{E} + (\vec{v} \times \vec{B})]$$             Only magnetic part: $$\vec{F}_m = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$
            Magnitude: $F = qvB\sin\theta$
            SI Unit of B: Tesla (T) or $Wb m^{-2}$        
       
            Note: Magnetic force is zero if charge is at rest or moving parallel to $\vec{B}$.        
       

2. Biot-Savart Law

       

Magnetic field due to current element.

       
            $$d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I d\vec{l} \times \hat{r}}{r^2}$$             $\mu_0$ = Permeability of free space = $4\pi \times 10^{-7} TmA^{-1}$        
       

Application 1: B due to Current Carrying Circular Loop

       
            At center: $$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$$
            At axial point: $$B = \frac{\mu_0 I R^2}{2(R^2 + x^2)^{3/2}}$$
            For $x >> R$: $$B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{2M}{x^3}$$ where $M = IA$        
       

Application 2: B due to Straight Wire

       
            $$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi r}(\sin\phi_1 + \sin\phi_2)$$
            For infinite wire: $$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$        
       

3. Ampere's Circuital Law

       

Line integral of magnetic field around closed loop = $\mu_0$ times net current enclosed.

       
            $$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enclosed}$$        
       

Application 1: B inside Solenoid

       
            $$B = \mu_0 n I$$
            $n$ = number of turns per unit length
            B is uniform inside, zero outside        
       

Application 2: B due to Toroid

       
            $$B = \mu_0 n I$$
            $n = \frac{N}{2\pi r}$, B is confined within toroid        
       

4. Force between Two Parallel Current Wires

       
            $$F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r} \times l$$             Force per unit length: $$f = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$
            Currents same direction → Attraction
            Currents opposite direction → Repulsion        
       

5. Torque on Current Loop in Uniform B

       
            $$\vec{\tau} = \vec{M} \times \vec{B}$$             $$|\tau| = N I A B \sin\theta = MB\sin\theta$$
            $M = NIA$ = Magnetic dipole moment
            Potential Energy: $U = -\vec{M} \cdot \vec{B} = -MB\cos\theta$        
       

6. Moving Coil Galvanometer

       

Device to detect/measure small current.

       
            Deflection: $$\theta = \left(\frac{NAB}{k}\right)I$$
            Current Sensitivity: $S_i = \frac{\theta}{I} = \frac{NAB}{k}$
            Voltage Sensitivity: $S_v = \frac{\theta}{V} = \frac{NAB}{kR}$
            $k$ = Torsional constant        
       

Conversion to Ammeter

       
            Shunt resistance: $$S = \frac{I_g R_g}{I - I_g}$$             Connected in parallel with galvanometer        
       

Conversion to Voltmeter

       
            High resistance: $$R = \frac{V}{I_g} - R_g$$             Connected in series with galvanometer

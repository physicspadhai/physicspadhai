Chapter 6: Electromagnetic Induction

               

1. Magnetic Flux

       

Number of magnetic field lines passing normally through a surface.

       
            $$\phi_B = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{A} = BA\cos\theta$$             SI Unit: Weber (Wb) or $Tm^2$
            $1 Wb = 1 Tm^2$        
       

2. Faraday's Law of EMI

       

EMF induced in a circuit is equal to rate of change of magnetic flux linked with it.

       
            $$e = -\frac{d\phi_B}{dt}$$             For N turns: $$e = -N\frac{d\phi_B}{dt}$$
            Negative sign = Lenz's Law        
       

3. Lenz's Law

       

Direction of induced current is such that it opposes the cause producing it.

       
            Note: Lenz's law is based on law of conservation of energy.        
       

4. Motional EMF

       

EMF induced in a conductor moving in magnetic field.

       
            $$e = Blv$$
            $l$ = length of conductor, $v$ = velocity perpendicular to B
            General case: $$e = (\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \cdot \vec{l}$$        
       

EMF Induced in Rotating Coil

       
            $$e = NBA\omega\sin\omega t$$
            Maximum EMF: $e_0 = NBA\omega$
            Instantaneous: $e = e_0\sin\omega t$        
       

5. Eddy Currents

       

Circular currents induced in bulk piece of conductor when magnetic flux linked changes.

       
            Applications: Induction furnace, Magnetic braking, Speedometer
            Disadvantage: Cause heating, power loss in transformers
            Minimized by: Using laminated cores        
       

6. Self Induction

       

Property of coil by virtue of which it opposes change of current flowing through it.

       
            $$\phi = LI$$ where $L$ = Self Inductance
            Induced EMF: $$e = -L\frac{dI}{dt}$$
            SI Unit of L: Henry (H)
            $1 H = 1 WbA^{-1} = 1 VsA^{-1}$        
       

Self Inductance of Solenoid

       
            $$L = \mu_0 n^2 Al = \frac{\mu_0 N^2 A}{l}$$
            $n$ = turns per unit length, $N$ = total turns        
       

Energy Stored in Inductor

       
            $$U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2$$        
       

7. Mutual Induction

       

Phenomenon of inducing EMF in a coil due to change of current in nearby coil.

       
            $$\phi_2 = MI_1$$ where $M$ = Mutual Inductance
            Induced EMF: $$e_2 = -M\frac{dI_1}{dt}$$
            SI Unit of M: Henry (H)        
       

Mutual Inductance of Two Solenoids

       
            $$M = \mu_0 n_1 n_2 Al$$
            For perfect coupling: $M = \sqrt{L_1 L_2}$
            Coefficient of coupling: $K = \frac{M}{\sqrt{L_1 L_2}}$, $0 \le K \le 1$        
       

8. AC Generator

       

Device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy.

       
            Principle: Electromagnetic Induction
            EMF: $$e = NBA\omega\sin\omega t$$
            Current: $$I = \frac{e}{R} = \frac{NBA\omega}{R}\sin\omega t = I_0\sin\omega t$$

Chapter 12: Atoms

               

1. Rutherford's Alpha Scattering Experiment

       

$\alpha$-particles fired at thin gold foil. Most passed, few deflected, 1 in 8000 bounced back.

       
            Conclusions:
            1. Atom mostly empty space
            2. Whole +ve charge & mass concentrated in nucleus
            3. Nucleus size $\approx 10^{-15}m$, Atom size $\approx 10^{-10}m$        
       

Distance of Closest Approach:

       
            $$r_0 = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{2Ze^2}{K}$$
            $K$ = K.E of $\alpha$-particle        
       

Impact Parameter:

       
            $$b = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{Ze^2\cot(\theta/2)}{K}$$        
       
            Drawback: Could not explain stability of atom & line spectrum. Accelerated electron should radiate energy and fall into nucleus.        
       

2. Bohr's Model of Hydrogen Atom

       

Postulates:

       
            1. Electrons revolve in stable orbits without radiating energy
            2. Angular momentum quantized: $$mvr = n\frac{h}{2\pi} = n\hbar$$
            3. Energy radiated/absorbed when electron jumps: $$h\nu = E_i - E_f$$        
       

Radius of nth Orbit:

       
            $$r_n = \frac{\varepsilon_0 h^2}{\pi m e^2} \frac{n^2}{Z} = 0.53\frac{n^2}{Z} \text{Å}$$
            For H-atom, $Z=1$: $$r_n = 0.53n^2 \text{Å}$$        
       

Velocity of Electron:

       
            $$v_n = \frac{e^2}{2\varepsilon_0 h}\frac{Z}{n} = \frac{c}{137}\frac{Z}{n}$$
            For H, ground state: $v_1 = 2.18 \times 10^6 ms^{-1}$        
       

Energy of Electron:

       
            $$E_n = -\frac{me^4}{8\varepsilon_0^2 h^2}\frac{Z^2}{n^2} = -13.6\frac{Z^2}{n^2} eV$$
            For H-atom: $$E_1 = -13.6 eV$$, $$E_2 = -3.4 eV$$, $$E_3 = -1.51 eV$$
            $$E_{\infty} = 0$$, Ionization energy = $13.6 eV$        
       

Frequency & Time Period:

       
            $$f_n = \frac{v_n}{2\pi r_n} = \frac{me^4}{4\varepsilon_0^2 h^3}\frac{Z^2}{n^3}$$
            $$T_n = \frac{1}{f_n} \propto n^3$$        
       

3. Hydrogen Spectrum

       
            $$\frac{1}{\lambda} = RZ^2\left(\frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2}\right)$$
            $R = 1.097 \times 10^7 m^{-1}$ = Rydberg constant

4. Limitations of Bohr Model

       
            1. Failed for multi-electron atoms
            2. Could not explain fine structure, Zeeman effect, Stark effect
            3. Violates Heisenberg uncertainty principle

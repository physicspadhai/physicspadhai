Chapter 2: Electrostatic Potential and Capacitance

               

1. Electrostatic Potential

       

Work done in bringing a unit positive test charge from infinity to a point in electric field without acceleration.

       
            $$V = \frac{W}{q_0} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{q}{r}$$             SI Unit: Volt (V) or $JC^{-1}$
            Dimensional Formula: $[ML^2T^{-3}A^{-1}]$        
       
            Note: Potential is a scalar quantity. Potential at infinity is taken as zero.        
       

2. Potential Difference

       

Work done in moving unit positive charge from one point to another in electric field.

       
            $$V_B - V_A = \frac{W_{AB}}{q_0} = -\int_A^B \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}$$        
       

3. Relation between E and V

       
            $$\vec{E} = -\text{grad } V = -\frac{dV}{dr}\hat{r}$$             In Cartesian: $$E_x = -\frac{\partial V}{\partial x}, E_y = -\frac{\partial V}{\partial y}, E_z = -\frac{\partial V}{\partial z}$$        
       
            Important: Electric field is in the direction of decreasing potential.        
       

4. Potential due to Point Charge

       
            $$V = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{q}{r}$$        
       

Potential due to System of Charges

       
            $$V = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{q_i}{r_i}$$        
       

Potential due to Electric Dipole

       
            On axial line: $$V_{axial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{p\cos\theta}{r^2}$$
            For $\theta = 0°$: $$V = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{p}{r^2}$$
            On equatorial line: $V = 0$ because $\theta = 90°$        
       

5. Equipotential Surface

       

Surface on which potential is same at every point.

       
            Properties:
            1. No work is done in moving charge on equipotential surface.
            2. Electric field is always perpendicular to equipotential surface.
            3. Two equipotential surfaces never intersect.        
       

6. Potential Energy of System of Charges

       
            For two charges: $$U = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r}$$
            For three charges: $$U = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \left[\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}} + \frac{q_2 q_3}{r_{23}} + \frac{q_3 q_1}{r_{31}}\right]$$        
       

Potential Energy of Dipole in External Field

       
            $$U = -\vec{p} \cdot \vec{E} = -pE\cos\theta$$        
       

7. Capacitance

       

Ability of a conductor to store charge. Ratio of charge to potential.

       
            $$C = \frac{Q}{V}$$             SI Unit: Farad (F)
            $1 F = 1 CV^{-1}$
            Dimensional Formula: $[M^{-1}L^{-2}T^4A^2]$        
       

8. Parallel Plate Capacitor

       
            Without dielectric: $$C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$
            With dielectric: $$C = \frac{K\varepsilon_0 A}{d} = KC_0$$
            Where $K$ = Dielectric constant        
       

Energy Stored in Capacitor

       
            $$U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2}QV = \frac{Q^2}{2C}$$             Energy density: $$u = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$$        
       

9. Combination of Capacitors

       

Series Combination:

       
            $$\frac{1}{C_s} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \frac{1}{C_3} + ...$$             Charge same, Potential divides: $V = V_1 + V_2 + V_3...$        
       

Parallel Combination:

       
            $$C_p = C_1 + C_2 + C_3 + ...$$             Potential same, Charge divides: $Q = Q_1 + Q_2 + Q_3...$        
       

10. Dielectrics and Polarization

       

Non-conducting materials. When placed in external field, get polarized.

       
            Induced Electric field: $E_p = E_0 - E_{ind}$
            $$E = \frac{E_0}{K}$$ where $K$ = dielectric constant
            Electric Susceptibility: $\chi = K - 1$

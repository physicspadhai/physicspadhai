Chapter 8: Electromagnetic Waves

               

1. Displacement Current

       

Current produced due to time-varying electric field in space.

       
            $$I_d = \varepsilon_0 \frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$$             Where $\phi_E$ = Electric flux
            Need: Ampere's law fails for charging capacitor, so Maxwell added this.        
       

2. Modified Ampere's Circuital Law

       

Also called Ampere-Maxwell Law

       
            $$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0(I_c + I_d) = \mu_0 I_c + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$$             $I_c$ = Conduction current, $I_d$ = Displacement current        
       

3. Maxwell's Equations

       
            1. Gauss Law in Electrostatics: $$\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q}{\varepsilon_0}$$
            2. Gauss Law in Magnetism: $$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0$$
            3. Faraday's Law: $$\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d\phi_B}{dt}$$
            4. Ampere-Maxwell Law: $$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_c + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$$        
       

4. Electromagnetic Waves

       

Waves produced by accelerated charge. $\vec{E}$ and $\vec{B}$ are perpendicular to each other and to direction of propagation.

       
            Speed in vacuum: $$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}} = 3 \times 10^8 ms^{-1}$$
            In medium: $$v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon}} = \frac{c}{\sqrt{\mu_r\varepsilon_r}} = \frac{c}{n}$$
            Relation: $$E_0 = cB_0$$        
       
            Properties: Transverse nature, No medium required, Carry energy & momentum, Exert radiation pressure        
       

5. Energy Density of EM Waves

       
            Electric field energy density: $$u_E = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$$
            Magnetic field energy density: $$u_B = \frac{B^2}{2\mu_0}$$
            Total energy density: $$u = u_E + u_B = \varepsilon_0 E^2 = \frac{B^2}{\mu_0}$$
            $u_E = u_B$ in EM wave        
       

6. Electromagnetic Spectrum

       
            Order of increasing $\lambda$, decreasing f:
            Gamma rays → X-rays → UV → Visible → IR → Microwaves → Radio waves
            $c = f\lambda$ for all EM waves

Chapter 13: Nuclei

               

1. Atomic Mass Unit (amu)

       
            $$1 amu = \frac{1}{12}\text{mass of C-12 atom} = 1.66 \times 10^{-27} kg$$
            Energy equivalent: $$1 amu = 931.5 MeV$$ using $E = mc^2$        
       

2. Composition of Nucleus

       

Nucleus contains protons & neutrons called nucleons.

       
            $_Z X^A$: $Z$ = Atomic number = No. of protons
            $A$ = Mass number = No. of protons + neutrons
            $N = A - Z$ = No. of neutrons
            Isotopes: Same $Z$, different $A$. Ex: $_1 H^1, _1 H^2, _1 H^3$
            Isobars: Same $A$, different $Z$. Ex: $_1 H^3, _2 He^3$
            Isotones: Same $N$. Ex: $_1 H^3, _2 He^4$        
       

3. Size of Nucleus

       
            $$R = R_0 A^{1/3}$$
            $R_0 = 1.2 \times 10^{-15} m = 1.2 fm$
            Volume $\propto A$, Density constant $\approx 2.3 \times 10^{17} kg m^{-3}$        
       

4. Mass Defect & Binding Energy

       
            Mass Defect: $$\Delta m = [Zm_p + (A-Z)m_n] - M_{nucleus}$$
            Binding Energy: $$B.E = \Delta m c^2 = [Zm_p + (A-Z)m_n - M]c^2$$
            Binding Energy per Nucleon: $$B.E/nucleon = \frac{B.E}{A}$$        
       
            Note: $B.E/nucleon$ vs $A$ curve peaks at $A=56$ for Fe. Most stable. Fission for $A>56$, Fusion for $A<56$ releases energy.        
       

5. Nuclear Force

       
            Properties:
            1. Strongest force in nature
            2. Short range $\approx 10^{-15}m$
            3. Charge independent: p-p = p-n = n-n
            4. Spin dependent & non-central
            5. Saturable        
       

6. Radioactivity

       

Spontaneous emission of $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ from unstable nucleus.

       
            Law: $$\frac{dN}{dt} = -\lambda N$$
            $$N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$
            Half-life: $$T_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$$
            Mean life: $$\tau = \frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.44 T_{1/2}$$
            Activity: $$A = \lambda N = A_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$ Unit: Becquerel (Bq)        
       

Radioactive Decays:

       
            $\alpha$-decay: $_Z X^A \rightarrow _{Z-2} Y^{A-4} + _2 He^4$
            $\beta^-$-decay: $_Z X^A \rightarrow _{Z+1} Y^A + _{-1}e^0 + \bar{\nu}$
            $\beta^+$-decay: $_Z X^A \rightarrow _{Z-1} Y^A + _{+1}e^0 + \nu$
            $\gamma$-decay: $_Z X^{*A} \rightarrow _Z X^A + \gamma$        
       

7. Nuclear Fission

       

Splitting of heavy nucleus into two lighter nuclei.

       
            Ex: $_0 n^1 + _{92}U^{235} \rightarrow _{56}Ba^{141} + _{36}Kr^{92} + 3_0 n^1 + 200 MeV$
            Chain reaction: Controlled in reactor, Uncontrolled in atom bomb        
       

8. Nuclear Fusion

       

Combining of two light nuclei to form heavy nucleus.

       
            Ex: $_1 H^2 + _1 H^3 \rightarrow _2 He^4 + _0 n^1 + 17.6 MeV$
            Requires very high temp $\approx 10^7 K$. Occurs in Sun, Hydrogen bomb.
